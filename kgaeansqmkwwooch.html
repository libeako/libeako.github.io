here i list the most well known arguments pro the wage minimum; i debunk them individually:

'the wage minimum does not destroy jobs'

'the age minimum increases efficiency by motivating the employee to produce more and decreasing employee turn-over

this is true mainly only relatively [at the micro level] when comparing different jobs; but absolutely [at macro level] this effect goes away

forcing people to output more is not good but bad, for them too; we can assume that organically people work so hard as is optimal for them; then the wage minimum forces them up to a sub-optimal level

employers have local bargaining power 'advantage'

this works in the other direction too

to the extent that it is difficult for the employee to change city: it is also difficult for the employer to attract the employees back; difficulty of moving between cities does not stop the employees from moving, just slows the movement; slow leakages of employees are still unsustainable for the employer

pre-known advantages are calculated into the investment of the employer, thus pre-known advantages does not cause extra-profit for the employer

it is okay to rob from the employers for the employees [for example with wage minimum] because the employees will spend that extra money to buy stuff from the firms which is approximately the same set of actors as the employers

this is obviously absurd

as voluntary market exchange is not the opposite of forced gifting
