from the previous description you hopefully see that the prices and profitabilities set by the free market mechanism are correct; both in and outside of equilibrium; by correctness i mean that they lead to optimal economical behavior

important conclusion

if the prices are distorted from what the free market mechanism determines then the behavior that they lead to is sub optimal

the state often distort prices from their economically correct value; either intentionally [for example by price constrains [for example wage minimum]] or as a non intended consequence of some intended state action [typical example is taxing]; the economical actors will continue to base their decisions on self interest, but the prices then being distorted will lead and incentivise the actors to suboptimal behavior; thus the state decreases the efficiency of the economy
