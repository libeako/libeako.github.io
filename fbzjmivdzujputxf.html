this part contains alternatives of the 'state-selected regime type' concept; they are all bad in my opinion [not accurate or not useful or ambiguous or...]

general problems

most of them are ambiguously defined

some of them seem to require 'absolute' power

that is wrong

'absolute' is often incorrectly used as synonym of 'total' and i guess here too

to require totality is a stupid choice in the design of this concept

some of them at least partially lack the 'infinitly recursivity' problem

perhaps the most important characteristic of state-selectedness is that to get rid of it is an infinitely recursive task

some of them require the perpetrator to be an individual

concrete alternatives

'dictatorship'

loaded with the 'totality' problem

i guess it requires individuality of the perpetrator

according to Wikipedia: this word comes from latin and means a judge who got 'absolute' state power temporarily

temporarity excludes the 'infinitely recursivity' problem

it was not the highest position in the state

if we assume that it means something like "to dictate"

that is bad because dictating can be perfectly healthy, normal, moral and legitimate

example

employer dictates to voluntary employee

parent dictates to child

'authoritarian'

even wikipedia is ambiguous about it, mentioning both of these

something like statism

but that does not necessarily involve capturing the state for personal goals [at least not theoretically]

weakness of the competing candidates

but that may be caused by not the lord misusing the state power

'tyrant'

according to wikipedia this comes from an ancient latin word meaning illegitimate ruler

i feel that this is the closest to 'state-selectedness'

but illegitimate also is true for those who lie to their voters, which is a very different kind of problem than state-selectedness, it does not include the 'infinite recusivity' problem

'despot'

loaded with the 'totality' problem

requires individuality of the perpetrator

'hybrid regime'

usually used where 'partial state-selected' should be used

partiality as a hybrid between empty and full - this is wrong way of thought

partial state selectedness is the same type of phenomenon as total state-selectedness, the difference is only the depth, strictness, amount

examples

a 15 year old is a 'hybrid' between a 10 and a 20 year old?

2 dollars is a 'hybrid' amount of money between 1 and 3 dollars?
