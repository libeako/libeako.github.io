the escape speed can be derived easily based on

energy conservation

assumptions

M - the big mass to escape from

m - the small mass, which escapes

the big mass is concentrated in a point

r - the distance from the big mass

energy just needed to escape = work done over the way away

=

integral (over [r, infinity)) (x -> G * M * m / r ^ 2)

=

G * M * m * integral (over [r, infinity)) (x -> 1 / r ^ 2)

=

G * M * m / r

=

m * g * r

escape speed

= ((kinetic energy) * 2 / m) ^ (1/2)

=

= ((G * M * m / r) * 2 / m) ^ (1/2)

=

= ((G * M / r) * 2) ^ (1/2)

=

= ((g * r) * 2) ^ (1/2)
