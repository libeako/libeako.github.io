every individual owns itself

this is the fundamental property right

this is the most intimate and most important right

this is freedom

derived property rights

if the property of x generates y then x owns y too

an important special case:

a human worker x performs work by using only its own body; then all the products of that work is the property of x alone

ownership of an owned thing can change by and only by the will of the owner

'will' includes exchange, charity, inheritance

violating the right to give or take property right is a violation of the right of both parties [the would be giver and the would be taker] [both of them are harmed]

what matters is not a formal treaty between them but their willing to do the exchange; the formal treaty is only a technicality, a proof of their will

example: violating the right of someone to talk or to listen wiolates the right of both the talker and the listener

all rights are these property rights, no other right exists

if it did then it would contradict these property rights
