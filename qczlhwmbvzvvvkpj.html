self-ownership - the individual owns itself

this is the fundamental property right

this is the most intimate and most important right

this is freedom

derived property rights

if the property of x generates y then x owns y too

an important special case:

a human worker x performs work by using only its own body; then all the products of that work is the property of x alone

ownership of an owned thing can change by the will of the owner

'will' includes exchange, charity, inheritance

violating the right to give or take property right is a violation of the right of both parties [the would-be giver and the would-be taker]

they both have the right for what the other side would give them, even if the exchange does not happen

what matters is not a formal treaty between them but their will to do the exchange; the formal treaty is only a technicality, a proof of their will

example: violating the right of someone to talk or to listen violates the right of both the talker and the listener

all rights are these property rights, no other right exists

if it did then it would contradict these property rights

merging our property with something does not give us property-right over that something

not even if that something is not scarce

example

agriculturing some land does not give us property-right over that land; not even if that land was not used before

the agriculturer may still morally force away aliens from there, in defense of his product [but not in defense of the land]

if the land becomes scarce or if the usage of the land becomes contested then the user is morally obligated to pay rent to the society; the fact that it is difficult to arrange this payment does not make that moral right to cease
