proposition

if

s is a sequence

limit of (n -> norm (s (n + 1) / s n)) exists; lets name it by "l"

then

l > 1 => (series s) is divergent

l < 1 => (series s) is convergent

proof

l > 1 the components of the series grow, hence the series diverges

l < 1

the norms of Cauchy segments of the series are smaller then the Cauchy segments of the norms of the components [triangular inequality of the norm], which are smaller than of the geometric series, which converges

other names may be "Cauchy ratio test", "D'Alambert's test"
