this book is the most gentle, most introductory among category theory books, but still mathematically precise; to anyone serious about learning category theory i recommend to start with this book; but some parts in it are incorrect or difficult to understand in it; here i will list them and try to give some advise how to move over them

some problematic parts

i read version (2016 february 8)

product

the author introduces the product definition in a special way; he explains the intuition about it, develops the definition instead of just stating it; i appreciate this intent very much; but i feel that the particular development that the author presents is not totally okay

first, the author draws the attention to the difference between defining and implementing products; this is very good point and the presentation of it is also okay; but in the next section the author describes the "pairing schemes"; this section is very formal, labourous to work through and on the other side it does not feel okay to me; though perhaps i just did not understand it; also : i was perfectly okay to move on to the the categorical definition of product without understanding the pairing schemes topic without problem; for me the categorical definition is intuitive on its own : a product is something from which one can project the components and vice versa and these 2 directions are compatible [the diagram commutes]; the one significant difference from a set theoretical point of view is that in category theory cartesian product is not given to work with, hence 2-input functions are also not given; instead this in category theory we can assume that an object represents pairs of objects by having 2 arrows into the components [forming a "wedge"]

pullback

introduction

the author suggests a connection between pullbacks and products; later important special cases of pullbacks are explained via this connection; hence i think this connection is important and should be more explicit in the book

wikipedia gives some hints

the pullbacks can be decomposed into a pair of such a product and a 3d object in the pullback; the opposite direction also works : each product with a suitable 3rd object forms a pullback; this could be a theorem; i proved it; the proof is easy; but perhaps i just made a mistake in it
