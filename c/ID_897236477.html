here by "democracy" i mean this word's original meaning : people's power it does not necessarily mean ruling by majority voting you can think about it as the absence of tyranny

let us define 'tyrant' as a ruler who uses its state power to support its position in the state

this definition is great in catching the main trait of tyranny - that it is a

vicious circle

the more power a ruler has - the better it can support its power with it as time passes - the less inhibition is in it, the more crime it has committed [without its legal power - would get into jail] thus as time passes - the tyrant's incentive [and usually ability] to stay in power grows - the tyranny gets deeper

this makes tyranny very dangerous

chicken and egg problem

in order to remove the tyran from the state - the tyrant's power should be decreased in order to decrease the power of the tyran - the tyrant must be removed from the state

this makes the tyranny very difficult to get rid of after it is established

according to this definition - tyranny is not 2-valued, either zero or total, but any value between the 2 extremes is possible

the lack of tyranny is 'democracy', which literally means that the state is ultimately in the hands of the ordinary people
