the rate

the income tax considered to be the compensation of the services by the state to the humans : its progression < linear

the economical decision are optimal when prices accurately mirror the production cost and usage value of the goods shortly : when the prices are fair in a free market this happens spontaneously not when the good is provided by the state still : let us try to estimate the production cost and usage value of the services of the state

production cost

the state does not spend more on human with higher income even the opposite is true higher income people on average commit less crime, take less state health care, state charity, state attorney, ...

usage value

the usage value of a service by the state does not grow faster than linear as the income grows

how the sub-optimality hurts the economy

with a more than linear progressivity, the state destroys [some of] the extra income that people get for beeing more productive it destroys [some of] the natural and healthy incentive to gravitate toward more productive activities no reason to work hard, be ambitious, take more responsibility, do unpleasant, unhealthy, risky work, ... if it is not reflected in the income
