the Newtonian formulation of classical mechanics is great, by its simplicity and intuitiveness; but it is too difficult to use in complex situations [complex constraints]

definition og the "Lagrangian"

L := K - P

equation of motion

slope (along x) L = slope (slope (along v) L)

equivalen to Newton' second law

proof

this is a variant of the mathematical Euler differential equation on

L (t, d, v) = m * v^2 / 2 - U d

d depending on t

v being the slope of d depending on t

hence the name "Euler-Lagrange equation" of this physical variant

hence

the path of the object of interest is where the integral of the Lagrangian is stationary

conservation of total momentum in some of the coordinates falls out trivially from the equation of motion and the supposition that the Lagrangian does not explicitly depend on those coorindates

the integral of the lagrangian is said to be the "action"

the action being a line-integral does not depend on the parameterization of the line; hence by equivalence the Euler-Lagrange equation is true for all parameterizations too [for all coordinate systems with full independence among its coordinates]

this is the big advantage of the Lagrangian formulation of classical mechanics
