the Borel sets are the smallest sigma algebra of sets including the open sets [or all the closed sets]

in 1-dimensional space

this is equivalent to the sigma algebra generated by the (-infinity, l], (l, k] type of intervals

the proof of these equivalences is easy

in Real the set of open sets are the unions of the open intervals

this can be thought of as induced by the standard metric space on Real

to prove the countability of them: use the countability of the rational numbers

in multi-dimensional space the same story is true

rectangles and halfspaces can both be used as alternative generators of the multi-dimensional Borel sigma algebra

the Lebesgue space is the completion of the Borel space
